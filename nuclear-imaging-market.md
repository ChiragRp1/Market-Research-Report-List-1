<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The nuclear imaging market comprises three main types of imaging systems: Positron Emission Tomography (PET), Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT), and Planar Scintigraphy systems. PET uses a radiotracer to produce detailed 3D images of organs and tissues, enabling early disease detection and better treatment planning. SPECT utilizes gamma cameras to create 3D images by measuring the gamma radiation emitted by a radioactive substance injected into the patient. Planar scintigraphy systems involve capturing 2D images by detecting gamma radiation from the patient's body using a gamma camera. These systems aid in diagnosing various medical conditions accurately.</p></p>
