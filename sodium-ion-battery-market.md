<p><p>Sodium-ion batteries are categorized into different types based on their compositions and mechanisms. Sodium-Sulfur batteries use solid-state electrodes, making them suitable for high-temperature applications like electric vehicles. Sodium-Salt batteries, also known as Zebra batteries, use molten salts as electrolytes, providing high energy density and long cycle life. Sodium-Oxygen (Sodium Air) batteries utilize oxygen from the atmosphere, offering a higher energy density and longer lifespan. These sodium-ion battery types present promising alternatives to traditional lithium-ion batteries, especially in energy storage applications.</p></p>
