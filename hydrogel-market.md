<p><p>The hydrogel market can be categorized into two types: natural macromolecule hydrogel and synthetic macromolecule hydrogel. Natural macromolecule hydrogels are produced from naturally occurring polymers, such as proteins and polysaccharides. These hydrogels have high biocompatibility and are commonly used in biomedical applications. On the other hand, synthetic macromolecule hydrogels are artificially created by chemically modifying polymers. These hydrogels offer greater control over their physical and chemical properties, making them suitable for various industrial applications such as agriculture, wastewater treatment, and drug delivery systems.</p></p>
