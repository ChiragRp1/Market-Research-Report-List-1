<p><p>In the commercial power line communication market, there are two main types: narrowband and broadband. Narrowband refers to the usage of a narrow frequency range to transmit signals, typically used for simple applications such as remote meter reading or streetlight control. On the other hand, broadband refers to the utilization of a wide frequency range, allowing for higher data transmission rates and more complex applications like smart grid management or home automation. Both narrowband and broadband power line communication systems offer unique benefits and cater to different communication needs within the commercial sector.</p></p>
