<p><p>Nitrogenous fertilizers are classified into various types based on their composition and usage in agriculture. Urea is a commonly used nitrogenous fertilizer with high nitrogen content. Ammonium nitrate is another prevalent type known for its suitability for crops during colder seasons. Ammonium sulfate is preferred for acidic soils as it lowers the pH level. Calcium ammonium nitrate (CAN) is a combination of ammonium nitrate and limestone that provides a gradual supply of nitrogen and calcium to plants. These fertilizers play a crucial role in enhancing crop growth and productivity.</p></p>
