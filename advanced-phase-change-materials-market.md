<p><p>Advanced Phase Change Materials (PCMs) are used in various industries to store and release thermal energy. These materials can be categorized into three types: organic, inorganic, and bio-based. </p><p>Organic PCMs are derived from hydrocarbon-based sources and offer high energy storage capacity and good thermal stability. Inorganic PCMs, on the other hand, are typically made from salt hydrates or metals and exhibit higher thermal conductivities. Lastly, bio-based PCMs are derived from renewable sources such as oils and fats, making them more sustainable and environmentally friendly. Each type of PCM has its own unique properties and applications in different industries.</p></p>
