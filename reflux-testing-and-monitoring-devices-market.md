<p><p>Reflux testing and monitoring devices are used to diagnose and monitor gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). These devices include reflux capsules, reflux probes, and reflux catheters. </p><p>A reflux capsule is a small device that is swallowed and measures the amount of acid in the esophagus. It transmits data to a recorder worn by the patient.</p><p>A reflux probe is a thin tube that is inserted through the nose into the esophagus. It measures the pH level and provides information on acid reflux.</p><p>A reflux catheter is a flexible tube that is also inserted through the nose. It measures acid in the esophagus and can detect episodes of acid reflux.</p></p>
