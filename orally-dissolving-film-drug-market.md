<p><p>The orally dissolving film drug market consists of two types: orodispersible film and oromucosal film. Orodispersible film is a thin strip that rapidly dissolves in the mouth when it comes into contact with saliva. It is usually used for delivering medications that require quick absorption. On the other hand, oromucosal film is also a thin strip that is placed on the mucous membranes of the mouth. It is designed to deliver drugs for local effects in the oral cavity, such as for treating gum diseases or mouth infections. Both types offer convenient and effective options for drug delivery.</p></p>
