<p><p>Natural Fiber Reinforced Composites (NFRC) are composite materials made by combining natural fibers with a polymer matrix, typically biodegradable and renewable. NFRC offer several benefits including high strength-to-weight ratio, low cost, low density, good thermal insulation, and less environmental impact compared to synthetic fibers.</p><p>The global market for Natural Fiber Reinforced Composites has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. <p><p>The natural fiber reinforced composites market can be divided into two main types: wood fiber composites and non-wood fiber composites. Wood fiber composites are composed of materials such as wood flour or wood particles combined with a resin matrix. These composites offer good strength and stiffness properties and are commonly used in the construction, automotive, and furniture industries. On the other hand, non-wood fiber composites use natural fibers derived from sources like hemp, flax, or kenaf. These composites are lightweight and environmentally friendly, making them suitable for applications in sectors like aerospace, packaging, and textiles.</p></p>
