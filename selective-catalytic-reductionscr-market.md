<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is a technology used to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions in diesel engines. This system works by injecting a reductant, typically ammonia or urea, into the exhaust stream before it enters the catalytic converter. The exhaust gases then pass over a catalyst, which triggers a reaction between the NOx and the reductant, converting it into harmless nitrogen and water.</p><p>The SCR market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years due to increasing government regulations regarding emissions control and the rising demand for energy-efficient vehicles. The automotive industry is the largest end-user of SCR technology, as it helps in reducing NOx emissions and complying with stringent emission standards.</p><p>Additionally, the growth of the industrial sector, specifically in power plants, cement manufacturing, and chemical industries, is driving the demand for SCR systems to control emissions. The implementation of stringent emission norms by regional authorities is further propelling the market growth.</p><p>The market analysis suggests that the Asia-Pacific region is expected to dominate the SCR market due to the presence of major automobile manufacturers and increasing industrial activities. <p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is an emission control technique used in diesel engines to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. NH3-SCR, Urea-SCR, and Ammonia-SCR are three types of SCR markets. NH3-SCR uses anhydrous ammonia as the reducing agent, Urea-SCR uses urea solution, and Ammonia-SCR utilizes ammonia gas to convert NOx into nitrogen and water. These techniques involve injecting the respective reducing agents into the exhaust gas stream, which reacts with NOx over a catalyst to break it down into harmless components, thus reducing emissions.</p></p>
