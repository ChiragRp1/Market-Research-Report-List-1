<p><p>The skin replacement and substitute market includes various types of products. Allografts are skin grafts from a donor of the same species. Xenografts involve grafts from a different species. Cultured epithelial autografts are artificially created grafts using a patient's own cells. Collagen scaffolds provide a framework for skin regeneration. Acellular dermal matrix products are derived from human or animal skin without cells. Amniotic membrane grafts utilize placental tissue. Synthetic skin products are man-made substitutes for natural skin. These various products serve as alternatives for patients needing skin replacement or repair.</p></p>
