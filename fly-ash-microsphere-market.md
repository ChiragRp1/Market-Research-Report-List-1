<p><p>Fly ash microspheres, also known as cenospheres, are lightweight, hollow spheres comprised mostly of silica and alumina. They are created as a byproduct of coal combustion in thermal power plants and are collected from the flue gas stream. These microspheres have a density of about 0.4-0.8 g/cc, making them ideal for various applications such as lightweight concrete, insulation materials, and oil drilling.</p><p>The fly ash microsphere market is witnessing significant growth due to increasing demand for lightweight materials in various industries. <p><p>Fly Ash Microspheres are lightweight, hollow or solid particles sourced from coal combustion byproducts. Hollow Fly Ash Microspheres have a low-density structure, enabling their usage as fillers in composites, insulation materials, and lightweight concrete. They enhance strength and reduce weight in these applications. On the other hand, Solid Fly Ash Microspheres are denser and possess higher compressive strength, making them suitable for applications like oil & gas well cementing, grouts, and coatings, providing improved chemical resistance and toughness. Both types find extensive use across various industries due to their unique properties.</p></p>
