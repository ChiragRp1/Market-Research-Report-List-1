Internal gear pumps consist of rotating gears that create suction, while external gear pumps have two interlocking gears to push fluid. Rotary vane pumps use vanes and a rotor to create suction and deliver fluid. Screw pumps have a helical screw that pushes fluid through the pump. These pumps are utilized in lubrication applications to ensure proper lubrication of machinery components, reducing friction and wear for efficient operation.</p></p>
