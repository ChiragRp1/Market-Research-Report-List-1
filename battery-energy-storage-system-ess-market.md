<p><p>Battery Energy Storage Systems (ESS) are electronic devices that store electrical energy and release it when needed. There are three main types of battery technologies used in ESS: lithium, lead acid, and sodium sulfur (NaS). Lithium batteries are widely used due to their high energy density and longer lifespan. Lead acid batteries, although less efficient and shorter-living, are cheaper and commonly used in renewable energy systems. NaS batteries, with high energy density and operating temperature, are suitable for large-scale grid storage applications. Each type has its advantages and disadvantages depending on the specific requirements and applications.</p></p>
