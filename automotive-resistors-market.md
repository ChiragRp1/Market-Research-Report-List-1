Shunt resistors are typically installed in high current circuits to measure or monitor the current passing through them. Voltage limiting resistors, on the other hand, are utilized to prevent excessive voltage levels that may damage sensitive components. Additionally, there are other types of resistors available in the automotive market, such as wire-wound resistors, ceramic resistors, and film resistors, chosen based on specific circuit requirements. These resistors play a crucial role in maintaining the proper functioning and safety of automotive electrical systems.</p></p>
