<p><p>Ophthalmology Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are devices used in eye surgeries to coagulate, cut, and seal tissue using electrical energy. Monopole Electrosurgical Units are equipped with a single active electrode and are used for general tissue cutting and coagulation. Bipolar Electrosurgical Units have two active electrodes and are used for precise coagulation in delicate eye surgeries. Vessel Sealing Units are designed specifically for sealing blood vessels during ophthalmic procedures, preventing bleeding and minimizing tissue damage. These different types cater to the specific needs of ophthalmic surgeons during various surgical interventions.</p></p>
