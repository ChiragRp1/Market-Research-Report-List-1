<p><p>The superabsorbent material market offers various types of production methods, including inverse suspension polymerization and aqueous solution polymerization. In inverse suspension polymerization, the monomer is dispersed in a non-aqueous continuous phase with a water-immiscible solvent. Through reactions, the monomer converts into a gel-like substance with absorbent properties. Aqueous solution polymerization involves dissolving the monomer in an aqueous solvent, which initiates polymerization to form a hydrogel network. Both methods result in the production of superabsorbent materials, which have high water-absorbing capacities and find applications in various industries.</p></p>
