<p><p>Biodiesel catalysts play a crucial role in the production of biodiesel fuel. There are two main types of catalysts used in this process: hydroxide catalysts and sodium methylate catalysts. Hydroxide catalysts, such as sodium hydroxide, are commonly used for batch processes and require a longer reaction time. Sodium methylate catalysts, on the other hand, offer faster reaction times and are often used in continuous processes. Both catalysts contribute to the conversion of vegetable oils or animal fats into biodiesel fuel by facilitating the esterification and transesterification reactions.</p></p>
