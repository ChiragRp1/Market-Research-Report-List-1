<p><p>The car steering column lock market consists of two main types: mechanical steering column locks (MSCL) and electronic steering column locks (ESCL). MSCL utilizes traditional mechanical systems to lock the steering column, often requiring a physical key to engage or disengage the lock. On the other hand, ESCL relies on electronic systems that use various technologies such as RFID or biometric authentication to lock and unlock the steering column. Both types serve the purpose of securing the steering column and preventing unauthorized access to the vehicle.</p></p>
