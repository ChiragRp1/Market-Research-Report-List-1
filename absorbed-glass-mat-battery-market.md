<p><p>An Absorbed Glass Mat (AGM) battery is a type of valve-regulated lead-acid (VRLA) battery that uses a glass mat separator to absorb and immobilize the electrolyte solution. Unlike traditional flooded lead-acid batteries, the AGM design eliminates the need for regular maintenance, as the electrolyte is held captive in the glass mat separator. AGM batteries are known for their superior performance and durability, making them ideal for various applications including automotive, motorcycles, recreational vehicles, and renewable energy systems.</p><p>The future outlook of the AGM battery market is quite promising. With the increasing demand for reliable power storage solutions across industries, the AGM battery market is expected to witness significant growth. Type I AGM batteries have thinner plates and separators, which provide higher energy density and are suitable for applications requiring high power output in a short time. On the other hand, Type II AGM batteries feature thicker plates and separators, offering enhanced durability and longer service life. These batteries are widely used in diverse industries, such as automotive, marine, and telecommunications, to meet specific power requirements.</p></p>
