<p><p>Splicing tapes are used in various industries for joining or splicing materials together. The market for splicing tapes can be categorized into two types: repulpable and non-repulpable. Repulpable tapes are designed to dissolve in water, making them suitable for applications where recycling of the spliced material is necessary. Non-repulpable tapes, on the other hand, do not dissolve in water and are commonly used in applications where recycling is not a concern. Both types offer strong adhesion and reliable performance, but their different properties cater to specific industry needs.</p></p>

<p><p>Splicing tapes find various applications in different sectors of the market. Flying splices are used for joining two moving webs continuously without stopping the production line. Manual splices involve manually joining two webs together during a production process. Core starting tapes are used for attaching new rolls to the core in order to continue the production without any interruption. Roll finishing tapes are applied at the end of a roll to secure and protect it during storage or transportation. These different types of splicing tapes cater to the specific needs of the market.</p></p>
