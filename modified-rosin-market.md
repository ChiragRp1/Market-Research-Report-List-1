<p><p>The modified rosin market includes various types of modified rosin products that serve different purposes in different industries. Hydrogenated rosin is obtained by hydrogenating natural rosin, resulting in enhanced stability and resistance to oxidation. Disproportionated rosin is produced by manipulating rosin with alkalis, resulting in varied properties for specific applications. Polymerized rosin is manufactured by polymerization of rosin to improve its adhesion and heat resistance. Acrylic modified rosin involves adding acrylic modifiers to increase durability and flexibility. Lastly, other types of modified rosin refer to unique formulations tailored to specific market needs.</p></p>
